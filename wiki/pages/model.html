Computational Framework

We have established a comprehensive computational biology framework specifically designed for plastic degradation enzyme design and optimization. This framework integrates various advanced computational tools and algorithms to provide comprehensive computational support for enzyme engineering.

Architecture Design

The computational framework adopts a modular design, containing the following core components:

  • Sequence Analysis Module: For protein sequence alignment, conservation analysis, and functional domain identification
  • Structure Prediction Module: Deep learning-based protein three-dimensional structure prediction
  • Dynamics Simulation Module: Molecular dynamics simulation and free energy calculation
  • Optimization Algorithm Module: Genetic algorithms and machine learning-driven enzyme design optimization

Technology Stack

Our computational framework is built on the following technology stack:

  • Python ecosystem (NumPy, SciPy, Pandas) for data processing and analysis
  • PyTorch deep learning framework for neural network model development
  • GROMACS and AMBER for molecular dynamics simulation
  • RDKit for small molecule and chemical reaction processing
  • Biopython for bioinformatics analysis

Molecular Modeling

Molecular modeling is a key technology for understanding enzyme catalytic mechanisms and guiding enzyme design. We adopt multi-scale modeling approaches, from quantum mechanics to molecular dynamics, to comprehensively describe enzyme behavior.

Protein Structure Modeling

Building three-dimensional structural models of enzymes using a combination of homology modeling and ab initio folding prediction methods:

  • Using SWISS-MODEL and Modeller for homology modeling
  • Employing AlphaFold2 and ESMFold for ab initio structure prediction
  • Optimizing structural models through molecular dynamics simulation
  • Validating and correcting computational models using experimental data

Enzyme-Substrate Complex Modeling

Accurate modeling of enzyme-plastic substrate interactions is crucial for understanding catalytic mechanisms:

  • Building molecular models of plastic polymers
  • Predicting binding modes between enzyme active sites and substrates
  • Analyzing the roles of key amino acid residues
  • Calculating binding free energy and reaction pathways

Quantum Chemical Calculations

Using quantum chemical methods at enzyme active sites to accurately describe chemical reactions:

  • DFT calculations to optimize active site geometric structures
  • Calculating reaction pathways and transition states
  • Analyzing electronic structure and chemical bond changes
  • Predicting catalytic activity and selectivity

AI-Driven Optimization

Artificial intelligence technology has brought revolutionary changes to enzyme engineering. We have developed various AI models to predict and optimize enzyme performance.

Deep Learning Models

Protein property prediction models based on deep neural networks:

  • Sequence-to-Structure Prediction: Predicting protein three-dimensional structure from amino acid sequences
  • Structure-to-Function Prediction: Predicting enzyme activity and stability from structural features
  • Mutation Effect Prediction: Predicting the impact of amino acid mutations on enzyme performance
  • Substrate Specificity Prediction: Predicting enzyme catalytic activity for different substrates

Machine Learning Algorithms

Employing various machine learning algorithms for enzyme design optimization:

  • Random Forest and Support Vector Machines for classification and regression tasks
  • Bayesian optimization for efficient search of optimal parameters
  • Reinforcement learning for enzyme design strategy optimization
  • Ensemble learning methods to improve prediction accuracy

Evolutionary Algorithms

Algorithms simulating natural evolutionary processes for enzyme sequence optimization:

  • Genetic algorithms for global optimization search
  • Differential evolution algorithms for optimizing enzyme design parameters
  • Particle swarm optimization algorithms for exploring design space
  • Multi-objective optimization to balance different performance metrics

Structure Analysis Methods

In-depth analysis of protein structural features is crucial for understanding enzyme catalytic mechanisms and guiding design optimization.

Active Site Analysis

Systematic analysis of enzyme active site structural features and dynamic behavior:

  • Identification and characterization of enzyme active sites
  • Analysis of active site geometric features and charge distribution
  • Study of active site dynamic behavior and conformational changes
  • Comparison of similarities and differences between different enzyme active sites

Protein Dynamics Analysis

Studying protein dynamic properties through molecular dynamics simulation:

  • Analysis of protein global motion patterns
  • Identification of important conformational changes and transition states
  • Study of temperature and pH effects on protein structure
  • Calculation of protein stability and folding pathways

Prediction Models

Developing accurate and reliable prediction models is the core objective of computational enzyme engineering. We have established various prediction models to guide enzyme design and optimization.

Enzyme Activity Prediction

Machine learning-based enzyme activity prediction models:

  • Training datasets containing extensive enzyme activity experimental data
  • Extracting protein sequence, structure, and physicochemical property features
  • Building prediction models using deep neural networks
  • Model validation and performance evaluation

Thermal Stability Prediction

Predicting enzyme stability under different temperature conditions:

  • Analyzing structural factors affecting protein thermal stability
  • Building prediction models based on physicochemical features
  • Validating model accuracy across different enzyme families
  • Guiding mutation design for thermal stability improvement

Substrate Specificity Prediction

Predicting enzyme catalytic specificity for different plastic substrates:

  • Establishing quantitative models of enzyme-substrate interactions
  • Analyzing the impact of substrate structure on catalytic activity
  • Predicting enzyme degradation capability for novel plastic materials
  • Optimizing enzyme substrate spectrum and selectivity

Validation Pipeline

Establishing a comprehensive computational validation pipeline to ensure the reliability and practicality of design solutions.

Computational Validation

Multi-level computational validation ensures model accuracy:

  • Structural validation: Validating the rationality and stability of predicted structures
  • Dynamics validation: Validating protein behavior through molecular dynamics simulation
  • Thermodynamic validation: Calculating and validating thermodynamic feasibility of reactions
  • Kinetic validation: Predicting and validating reaction kinetic parameters

Experimental Design Guidance

Computational results guide experimental design and optimization:

  • Predicting the most promising mutants for experimental validation
  • Optimizing experimental conditions and parameters
  • Designing control experiments and validation strategies
  • Analyzing experimental results and providing feedback to optimize computational models

Iterative Optimization

Establishing iterative optimization cycles between computation and experiment:

  • Updating and improving computational models based on experimental results
  • Training more accurate prediction models using new experimental data
  • Continuously optimizing computational methods and algorithms
  • Achieving deep integration of computation and experimentation

Through this comprehensive dry lab design methodology, we can provide full computational support for plastic degradation enzyme engineering from a computational perspective, greatly improving the success rate and efficiency of enzyme design, and providing strong theoretical guidance for wet lab experiments.
