Laboratory Safety

We strictly follow international standard biosafety protocols to ensure all experimental activities are conducted in a safe and controlled environment. All team members have received professional safety training and are familiar with various safety operating procedures.

Biosafety Level

Our experimental work is conducted in BSL-1 (Biosafety Level 1) laboratories, complying with safety requirements for handling non-pathogenic microorganisms:

  • The E. coli strains used are standard laboratory strains with no pathogenicity
  • All experimental operations follow standard microbiological laboratory practices
  • The laboratory is equipped with necessary safety equipment and protective gear
  • Waste disposal strictly follows biosafety waste management protocols

Personal Protective Equipment

All personnel entering the laboratory must wear appropriate personal protective equipment:

  • Lab coats: Prevent clothing contamination, must be removed before leaving the laboratory
  • Safety goggles: Protect eyes from chemical reagent splashes
  • Gloves: Changed regularly during use, replaced immediately when contacting different reagents
  • Closed-toe shoes: Prevent accidental spills from causing foot injuries

Risk Assessment

We have conducted comprehensive risk assessments for all biological materials, chemical reagents, and experimental procedures involved in our project.

Biological Risk Assessment

Detailed assessment of biological materials used:

Host Cell Safety

  • E. coli DH5Î±: Standard cloning strain, non-pathogenic, widely used in molecular cloning
  • E. coli BL21(DE3): Protein expression strain, genetically modified to lose intestinal colonization ability
  • Both strains are approved by regulatory authorities for research purposes

Foreign Gene Safety

  • Expressed plastic degradation enzymes originate from known safe microorganisms
  • No pathogenic factors or antibiotic resistance genes introduced (except necessary selection markers for experiments)
  • All gene sequences analyzed through bioinformatics to confirm non-toxicity

Chemical Risk Assessment

Risk assessment of chemical reagents used in experiments:

  • Organic solvents: Such as ethanol, isopropanol, operated in fume hoods
  • Acid-base reagents: Such as HCl, NaOH, use protective equipment, prepare neutralizing agents
  • Protease inhibitors: PMSF etc., avoid skin contact, use in ventilated environments
  • DNA dyes: Use safe DNA dyes to replace ethidium bromide

Containment Measures

We have established multi-level containment measures to ensure that engineered microorganisms will not be accidentally released into the environment.

Physical Containment

  • Laboratory access control: Only trained personnel can enter the laboratory
  • Negative pressure system: Laboratory maintains negative pressure to prevent microbial spread
  • HEPA filtration: Ventilation systems equipped with high-efficiency air filters
  • Biosafety cabinets: Microbial operations conducted in biosafety cabinets

Biological Containment

  • Nutritional dependence: Engineered strains depend on specific nutrients for growth
  • Temperature sensitivity: Designed strains cannot survive at environmental temperatures
  • Kill switches: Consider introducing programmed cell death mechanisms
  • Genetic circuit control: Enzyme expression controlled by strict genetic regulatory systems

Waste Disposal

Strict waste disposal procedures ensure biosafety:

  • All waste containing microorganisms is sterilized by high-temperature, high-pressure autoclaving
  • Culture media and petri dishes must be thoroughly sterilized before disposal
  • Chemical waste is categorically collected according to chemical waste management protocols
  • Complete experimental records enable traceability of all biological material disposal

Environmental Impact Assessment

We have conducted in-depth analysis of the potential environmental impacts of our project and developed corresponding risk mitigation strategies.

Positive Environmental Impacts

  • Plastic pollution reduction: Effectively degrade plastic waste, reducing environmental burden
  • Resource recycling: Convert plastic waste into useful chemical raw materials
  • Reduced carbon footprint: Lower energy consumption compared to traditional plastic processing methods
  • Ecosystem protection: Reduce plastic harm to marine and terrestrial ecosystems

Potential Environmental Risks

Although our system design fully considers safety, potential risks still need to be assessed:

Microbial Release Risk

  • Accidental leakage: Establish emergency response procedures, equipped with disinfection equipment
  • Gene transfer: Use suicide vectors to reduce horizontal gene transfer
  • Ecological niche competition: Engineered strains have weak competitiveness in natural environments

Degradation Product Impact

  • Product toxicity: All degradation products are known safe compounds
  • Cumulative effects: Monitor behavior of degradation products in the environment
  • Ecotoxicological assessment: Conduct necessary ecotoxicity testing

Regulatory Compliance

We strictly comply with relevant laws, regulations, and international guidelines.

National Regulations

  • Follow relevant provisions of the Biosafety Law
  • Comply with requirements of the Pathogenic Microorganism Laboratory Biosafety Management Regulations
  • Obtain necessary laboratory qualifications and permits
  • Regularly accept inspections and audits from regulatory authorities

International Standards

  • Reference WHO Laboratory Biosafety Manual
  • Follow OECD Biotechnology Safety Guidelines
  • Comply with requirements of the Cartagena Protocol on Biosafety
  • Adopt OECD framework for safety assessment of transgenic organisms

Emergency Procedures

We have established comprehensive emergency response procedures to deal with possible accidents.

Accident Response Workflow

  1. Immediate control: Rapidly isolate the accident area to prevent spread
  2. Personnel safety: Ensure all personnel evacuate to safe areas
  3. Emergency response: Take appropriate response measures according to accident type
  4. Reporting and notification: Timely report accident conditions to relevant departments
  5. Follow-up tracking: Conduct accident investigation and environmental monitoring

Emergency Equipment Configuration

  • Emergency showers and eyewash stations
  • Fire fighting equipment and fire suppression systems
  • Emergency medicines and first aid kits
  • Spill response kits and disinfectants
  • Emergency communication equipment

Through this comprehensive safety management system, we ensure that research activities are conducted within a safe and responsible framework, minimizing risks to personnel health and the environment to the greatest extent, providing solid safety assurance for the successful implementation of our project.
