1. Problem Statement

The global plastic pollution crisis has become one of the most severe environmental challenges facing our world today. Over 8 million tons of plastic waste enter the oceans annually, causing irreversible damage to marine ecosystems. Traditional plastic treatment methods, such as incineration and landfilling, are not only inefficient but also generate harmful byproducts.

Existing plastic recycling technologies have numerous limitations: low recycling rates, high costs, enormous energy consumption, and inability to effectively process mixed plastic waste. These challenges urgently demand a revolutionary solution to fundamentally transform how we handle plastic waste.

2. Our Solution

XJTLU iGEM Team 2025 proposes an innovative biotechnology solution that achieves efficient plastic waste degradation through the development of AI-enhanced enzyme systems. Our approach combines cutting-edge synthetic biology technologies with artificial intelligence algorithms to create engineered microorganisms capable of specifically degrading particular types of plastics.

Our core strategies include:

  • Utilizing machine learning algorithms to optimize enzyme design and activity
  • Developing enzyme systems specifically targeting common plastics such as PET, PE, and PP
  • Constructing controllable and monitorable bioreactor systems
  • Achieving resource utilization of plastic degradation products

3. Innovation

Our project achieves breakthrough innovations in multiple aspects:

3.1 AI-Driven Enzyme Engineering

By analyzing the relationship between protein structure and function through deep learning algorithms, we can design plastic degradation enzymes with higher activity and stronger stability. This approach significantly reduces enzyme optimization time and improves design precision.

3.2 Multi-Enzyme Synergistic System

We have developed a multi-enzyme synergistic degradation system capable of simultaneously processing multiple types of plastics. Through carefully designed metabolic pathways, different enzymes can work synergistically to maximize degradation efficiency.

3.3 Intelligent Monitoring System

Integrated sensors and data analysis systems can monitor the degradation process in real-time, automatically adjust reaction conditions, and ensure optimal degradation effects.

4. Methodology

Our research adopts a multidisciplinary and interdisciplinary approach:

4.1 Computational Modeling and Simulation

Using molecular dynamics simulations and quantum chemical calculations to predict interactions between enzymes and plastic molecules, guiding enzyme design and optimization.

4.2 Molecular Biology Experiments

Constructing expression vectors through genetic engineering techniques, expressing designed enzymes in E. coli, and conducting activity testing and optimization.

4.3 System Integration and Testing

Building complete bioreactor systems, testing plastic degradation effects under real conditions, and optimizing process parameters.

5. Expected Impact

Our project is expected to generate positive impacts at multiple levels:

5.1 Environmental Benefits

Significantly reduce plastic pollution, particularly marine plastic waste, protecting ecological environments and biodiversity.

5.2 Economic Benefits

Create new green industries, provide employment opportunities, while reducing the costs of traditional plastic treatment.

5.3 Social Benefits

Enhance public environmental awareness and promote the widespread adoption of sustainable development concepts.

6. Sustainability

Our project fully aligns with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, particularly:

  • Goal 6: Clean Water and Sanitation - Reducing plastic pollution in water bodies
  • Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production - Promoting plastic recycling
  • Goal 14: Life Below Water - Protecting marine ecosystems
  • Goal 15: Life on Land - Reducing terrestrial plastic pollution

By addressing plastic pollution through biotechnology, we not only provide an environmentally friendly solution but also make significant contributions to global sustainable development.
